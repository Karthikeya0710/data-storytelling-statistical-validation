# Data Storytelling & Statistical Validation

## Project Overview

This project combines findings from previous analysis tasks and presents them as a business story supported by statistical validation.

The objective was to summarize business insights, communicate findings clearly, and validate results using basic statistical testing.

---

## Business Objective

Analyze customer billing and tipping behavior, identify patterns, and present meaningful business insights for decision-making.

---

## Key Findings

- Higher total bills generally resulted in higher tips
- Weekend days generated more total revenue
- Customer tipping patterns varied by day
- Smoker and non-smoker behavior showed small differences

---

## Data Story

### Business Problem
Understand customer payment and tipping trends and identify business opportunities.

### Analysis Performed
- Data cleaning and preparation
- Exploratory Data Analysis
- KPI analysis
- Dashboard reporting

### Business Insights
- Weekend revenue is stronger than weekdays
- Higher-value bills create higher tip amounts
- Customer behavior differs across segments

### Recommended Action
- Focus promotions on high-revenue days
- Improve customer engagement during low-revenue periods
- Monitor customer segments regularly

---

## Hypothesis Testing

### Hypothesis
Higher bill amounts lead to higher tip values.

### Test Summary
A basic statistical comparison was performed.

### Result
Positive relationship observed between bill amount and tip.

### Business Conclusion
Customers spending more tend to leave larger tips.
